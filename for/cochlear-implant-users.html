For cochlear implant users

Alerts when

your processor is off.

A cochlear implant gives you sound during the hours your processor is on. The hours when it is off, showering, swimming, sleeping, charging, device malfunction, recovery from surgery, are the gap. SoundSense runs on your iPhone and fills that gap without asking anything of your implant.

The processor-off hours

Four scenarios.

Sleeping without the processor.

The hours between removing your processor at bedtime and putting it back on in the morning are exactly when you most need a smoke alarm to reach you. SoundSense runs on the phone on your nightstand, full-screen alerts for anything critical, wrist buzz if you wear a Watch.

Showering or swimming.

Bathroom moments are often when the doorbell rings, the fire alarm tests, or someone knocks. Your phone outside the shower can relay alerts visually if you keep it in view, and vibrate strongly enough to reach you through the towel or the swim bag.

Device malfunction.

When your processor has a battery failure, a coil issue, or is being serviced, you are effectively back to silence. SoundSense is a layer that does not depend on your implant at all, which means it is still there when your implant temporarily isn't.

Surgery and recovery.

After CI surgery, and in the weeks between activation and comfortable daily use, you are adapting. SoundSense gives you a visual-haptic safety net during that window without asking you to rely on an unfamiliar auditory setup for critical alerts.

A day in the life

How it fits.

Morning: you take your processor off the charger and put it on. Your day begins with your implant doing its job. You do not need SoundSense actively doing anything. It is there in the background, silent.

Midday: you decide to go for a swim. You leave the processor in a waterproof case on the pool deck along with your phone. If something happens at the pool, someone calls your name, a lifeguard whistle, your phone flashes and vibrates. You are not flying blind.

Evening: processor off, on the charger, nightstand set up. Phone on a wireless pad next to the bed, Apple Watch on your wrist. The smoke alarm fires at 3 AM. Your Watch buzzes full-urgency, your phone lights up, the alert does not stop until you dismiss it. You wake up. That is the whole design.

Get started

Three steps.

1. Install free

On your iPhone. Grant microphone and notification permissions. No account needed.

2. Enable safety sounds

Smoke alarm, CO alarm, doorbell, knock, name called. These are the five that matter most during processor-off hours.

3. Keep the phone near

On the nightstand, on the pool deck, in the bathroom. Within phone-vibration distance during off-hours is the setup that works.

Quick questions

If your processor supports MFi audio routing, iOS will let you route SoundSense's optional audio cues there. By default, however, SoundSense is visual and haptic, which means alerts still work when your processor is off. That is usually what CI users need.
