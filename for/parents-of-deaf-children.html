For parents of deaf children

Safety

without hovering.

Your child is building independence. You want them safe and you do not want to be their constant monitor. SoundSense gives them a sound awareness tool they can use themselves, with an optional (Pro-tier) path for you to get emergency alerts when it matters most.

Scenarios

When it is useful.

Home alone for the first time.

That milestone, first time they stay home while you run an errand, is easier when their phone knows the doorbell, the smoke alarm, and the knock at the door. They answer or they don't, but they are not caught off guard.

Sleepovers and babysitters.

A friend's house, a grandparent's apartment, a babysitter they do not know well. Your child's phone becomes the portable sound awareness layer: doorbell, smoke detector, someone calling their name, regardless of whose house they are in.

Family trips.

Hotel rooms, rental houses, Airbnbs: different doorbells, different alarm systems, different background sounds. SoundSense adapts to the environment without you having to install anything local.

Emergency-only caregiver alerts (Pro).

When SoundSense Pro launches, you will be able to opt in to receiving only the critical-tier alerts your child's phone generates, a smoke alarm, not a microwave beep. This is a safety net, not a surveillance tool. The child controls what counts as critical.

A day in the life

How it fits your family.

You help your child install SoundSense on their iPhone. Together, you enable the sounds that matter: smoke alarm, doorbell, fire alarm, their name. You show them how to train a custom sound. They do not need you to set anything else up; the default settings are sensible.

That afternoon, they are home alone for the first time. The doorbell rings. Their phone flashes. They check who is there, tell you later that it was a delivery. They handled it. You did not need to be there.

At bedtime, you say good night and trust that if a smoke alarm goes off at 3 AM, their phone will wake them. It will. That is the whole point. Eventually, when they are older and Pro is available, you might opt in to getting notified about critical alerts too: your choice, their choice, mutually agreed on rather than enforced.

Get started

Three steps,

together.

1. Install on their phone

The child is the primary user. The app runs on their device. Setup takes a few minutes and the two of you can walk through it together.

2. Train the critical sounds

Your smoke alarm, doorbell, and the sound of "name called" are the big three to start with. Train them in the actual house.

3. Check in after a week

Talk about what worked, what annoyed them, what they want to change. Their input drives every setting.
