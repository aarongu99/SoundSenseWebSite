Fill the gaps

in your hearing.

Hearing aids help. They do not catch everything. When your aids are out, when the environment is loud, when the sound is behind you: those are the moments a second layer of awareness earns its place. SoundSense is that second layer.

Scenarios

Where it earns its place.

Aids are out.

End of the day, drying the aids, running an errand without them. SoundSense takes over the doorbell, the phone, the kitchen. Your home stays audibly aware without your hearing aids being in your ears.

Late-night safety.

Aids off in bed, the house quiet. A smoke alarm, a window breaking, a knock at 2 AM: the sounds you most need to catch are the ones your aids are not there for. SoundSense runs on your phone on the nightstand.

Loud cafes, restaurants.

When the background is loud enough to strain your aids, SoundSense can flag the things that matter, someone calling your name, a direct doorbell or knock at the venue, on your wrist or phone, not through the auditory scene you are already struggling with.

Works alongside your aids.

If you use Made for iPhone hearing aids, SoundSense alerts can route there too. Visual and haptic alerts on your phone and Watch remain the primary channel, so you are not dependent on aids being in to get notified.

A day in the life

How it fits.

You put your aids in at the start of the day. You do not need SoundSense in the loud direct environment. Your aids are doing their job. But at lunch you are in a crowded restaurant, and when a friend calls your name from the door, your phone pulses: "Name called." You look up.

After dinner, you take your aids out to rest your ears. The doorbell rings; SoundSense catches it, your phone vibrates, you answer. Later, getting ready for bed, you drop your aids into their charger. You leave your phone nearby on the nightstand. If the smoke alarm fires at 3 AM, it will wake you: full-screen takeover, strongest haptic, wrist vibration if you wear your Watch to bed. You did not have to remember anything.

Get started

Three steps.

1. Install free

Grant mic and notifications. Takes under a minute.

2. Pick your gaps

Focus on the sounds your aids do not catch well. Doorbell, name called, safety sounds, alarms.

3. Keep your routine

Wear your aids the way you already do. SoundSense runs underneath, only surfacing what it finds.
