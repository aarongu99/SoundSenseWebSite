What it does for you

Four scenarios

from everyday life.

Waking up on time.

Your specific alarm, trained on three short samples, wakes you through your phone's strongest haptic and, with SoundSense+ on Apple Watch, a wrist vibration. No flashing-light rental gear required for travel.

Answering the door.

Train SoundSense on your exact doorbell: the specific chime your building has, not a generic category. A knock can be a separate trained sound. Both show up as visible alerts with your chosen urgency.

Kitchen safety.

Smoke alarm gets the full critical-alert treatment any time. Microwave beep is gentle in the kitchen, logged silently elsewhere. Oven timer, kettle whistle, your specific appliances: all trainable, all distinct.

Workplace meetings.

"Name called" detection picks up when someone calls your name across a room: in a meeting, at your desk, at a cafe table. Your phone vibrates, you turn around. Helpful when you are focused and not facing the speaker.

A day in the life

How it fits in.

In the morning, your alarm wakes you through strong haptics, or your Watch, or both. You check the overnight timeline to see that the smoke alarm never went off and nothing woke the building at 3 AM. You go through your morning without thinking about the app; it is running in the background.

Around lunch, the doorbell rings. Your phone vibrates with the distinctive pattern you chose for that sound. You answer the door or don't. Your choice. At the office or on your laptop at home, someone says your name. Your phone tells you; you turn. Small moments, but real ones.

Late afternoon, the microwave beeps. Low-urgency haptic, logged in the timeline. You pick up your food when you are ready. That night, someone knocks on the apartment door. The knock is trained; SoundSense knows it; you know about it. At 11 PM, you charge your phone and Apple Watch and go to bed, and the cycle starts again.

Get started

Three steps.

1. Install

Download free. Grant microphone and notification permissions. No account required.

2. Train your sounds

Pick presets that apply. Train your specific doorbell, alarm, appliances, and name.

3. Live with it

Carry your phone the way you already do. The app learns, the alerts get better, and you stay in control of every setting.
